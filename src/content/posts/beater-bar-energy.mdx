The first version of Spinup's weapon was a 3" steel beater. It looked terrifying.
It was, statistically, not very terrifying.

Energy of a spinning rod is `E = ½ · I · ω²`, with `I = (1/12) · m · L²` for a uniform
bar about its center. Length is squared. Doubling the length quadruples the moment of
inertia at the same mass — and energy scales linearly with `I`.

<Callout tone="fail" title="lesson 1">
  Picking weapon length by what *fits* in the chassis is how you end up with a bot
  that bumps opponents instead of cutting them.
</Callout>

Pit-damage threshold for a 3lb bot is roughly 50 J. Drag the chart's mass slider
and you'll see how quickly that line moves.
